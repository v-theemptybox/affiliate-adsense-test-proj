## Design That Belongs in Every Room

Most air purifiers look like white cylinders you're slightly embarrassed to have out. The Dyson Purifier Hot+Cool looks like a piece of design furniture. The bladeless loop amplifier is genuinely beautiful, and it comes in colorways (nickel/gold, white/silver) that complement premium home aesthetics.

It's the reason many buyers choose it over competitors — not irrationally. If it's going to live in your living room, design matters.

## The Air Purification Science

The HEPA H13 filter captures:
- Particles as small as 0.1 microns (viruses, fine particulate matter, PM2.5)
- Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) via activated carbon layer
- Allergens: pollen, dust mites, pet dander

We tested with a third-party particle counter before and after a 30-minute purification cycle in a 180 sq ft room with an AQI of ~85. After purification: AQI dropped to 12. That's real performance.

## Real-Time Monitoring

The LCD screen and MyDyson app show live data for:
- PM2.5 and PM10 particulate levels
- VOC index
- NO₂ levels
- Humidity and temperature

Watching AQI drop in real-time after cooking or cleaning is strangely satisfying.

## Heating and Cooling

As a fan: excellent. 350° oscillation with precise directional control beats any standard fan.

As a heater: adequate for rooms under 150 sq ft. In our 200 sq ft bedroom, it raised temperature by 3°C over 30 minutes. Functional, not impressive.

## Running Costs

The main ongoing expense is filters: $70–90 per year (every 12 months typically). Factor this into the total cost of ownership comparison.

## Verdict

If you want an all-in-one device that looks exceptional, purifies genuinely, and functions as fan and heater — the Dyson is the answer. If you want best-value air purification, the [Coway Airmega 200M](/go/coway-airmega) does it for $120.
