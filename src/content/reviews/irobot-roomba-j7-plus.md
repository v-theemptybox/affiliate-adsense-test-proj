## Six Weeks With the Roomba j7+

We tested the j7+ in a 1,400 sq ft apartment with two cats, hardwood floors, area rugs, and the usual collection of charging cables on the floor. Here's what actually happened.

## The Obstacle Avoidance Is Real

iRobot's PrecisionVision Navigation uses a front-facing camera to identify and avoid objects in real time. After years of robot vacuums eating charging cables and requiring constant babysitting, this actually works.

In six weeks of daily runs, the j7+ successfully avoided:
- 47 detected obstacles (cables, socks, a stray shoe)
- Cat toys scattered across the floor
- Two instances of cat vomit (the moment this category was invented for)

One false positive: it avoided a dark area rug edge on day 3, treating it as an obstacle. After remapping, this stopped.

## Self-Emptying Base

The Clean Base automatically empties the dustbin after each run into an AllergenLock bag that holds 60 days of debris. This is genuinely transformative — we went from manually emptying a robot vacuum every 2–3 days to touching the base once every two months.

The base is large and requires a dedicated spot near an outlet, but that's a reasonable trade.

## Mapping and Scheduling

The Roomba app lets you:
- Name rooms and set custom schedules per room
- Create "Keep Out Zones" for areas you want skipped
- Run specific rooms on demand ("clean the kitchen")

The map took about 3 full runs to stabilize. After that, navigation was efficient with minimal re-routing.

## Cleaning Performance

On hardwood: excellent. Picks up cat hair, fine dust, and larger debris in a single pass.

On area rugs: strong on low-pile, struggles slightly on thick shag (common across all robot vacuums at this price).

## Should You Buy It?

At $599, the j7+ isn't cheap. But if you value your time and have pets or kids, the math works. An hour of cleaning replaced by an automated daily cycle that you never need to think about is worth real money to most people.

If your home has no pets and mostly hard floors, the $299 Roomba i3+ performs 80% as well for half the price.
