Both robots cost around $450–600, both self-empty, both map your home. So which one do you actually buy?

## The Short Answer

**For obstacle avoidance and pet households** → Roomba j7+ wins.

**For carpet-heavy homes and pet hair** → Shark AI ULTRA wins.

## Obstacle Avoidance

The Roomba j7+ uses PrecisionVision to identify and avoid objects in real time. In our testing it correctly avoided cables, pet toys, and yes — pet waste — on every occasion.

The Shark AI ULTRA has improved obstacle avoidance in recent firmware updates but still occasionally runs over small items.

**Winner: Roomba j7+** — and it's not close.

## Carpet and Pet Hair

The Shark AI ULTRA's Matrix Clean pattern is methodical — it moves in overlapping rows rather than random bouncing, ensuring every part of the carpet is covered. For thick carpet with embedded pet hair, this matters.

**Winner: Shark AI ULTRA**

## Price and Value

The Shark is $150 cheaper. If obstacle avoidance isn't your priority and you have carpet, that's a meaningful difference.

**Winner: Shark AI ULTRA** on value

## Final Verdict

For most homes — especially those with pets and mixed flooring — the **Roomba j7+** is worth the premium. The obstacle avoidance alone prevents the frustration that makes people stop using robot vacuums entirely.

If you have primarily carpet and want thoroughness over smart avoidance, save $150 with the **Shark AI ULTRA**.
